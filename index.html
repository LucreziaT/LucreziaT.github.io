About me

I am a PhD candidate working on Remote Sensing Visual Question Answering (RSVQA) in Paris. My research is a collaboration between Université Paris Cité and ONERA, aiming to extract meaningful information from both optical and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) images. All my projects have a focus on interpretability, and lately also on explainability.

My research methods involve the fusion of image features with textual queries, employing techniques such as segmentation and attention mechanisms. The ultimate aim is to produce comprehensive responses by effectively combining visual data from different sensors with the contextual information embedded in the questions.

Beyond remote sensing, I am passionate about different fields in image processing and computer vision, including human behaviour analysis, forensic studies, sports analysis and medical studies. This multifaceted interest caused me to participate in several projects in these areas.
